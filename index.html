Privacy Policy

Calculator Vault · Last updated: April 21, 2026

This policy describes how the Calculator Vault application (“the app”) handles information on your device. By using the app, you agree to what is described here.

1. Storage and file permissions

The app’s core purpose is to encrypt, hide, and manage files locally. To do that, it may request access to device storage, including but not limited to:

  • MANAGE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE (“All files”): broad access when the system requires it to import or hide files from different folders, or to restore them reliably.
  • READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE (on older Android versions, typically up to API 32): read shared files so you can select them and move them into the app’s encrypted storage.
  • READ_MEDIA_IMAGES and READ_MEDIA_VIDEO (Android 13 and later): scoped access to photos and videos so you can choose what to protect.

This access is used only for vault operations (for example: import, encrypt, restore, or remove the original as the app’s flow requires). It is not used to read your files for purposes unrelated to that functionality.

2. Local data and no cloud vault

Your sensitive content stays on the device. Files you protect are encrypted and stored in the app’s private storage. Vault information (for example, metadata needed to organize folders and files) is handled locally using on-device databases.

Passwords, PINs, preferences, and notes you set within the app are processed locally; we do not upload your encrypted files, passwords, or vault content to our servers because we do not operate a cloud service for that content. The app is not designed to sync your vault to remote servers.

Your responsibility: if you lose the device or uninstall the app without exporting what you need first, access to that data may be lost depending on the system and Android backup settings.

3. Third-party services (crash diagnostics)

To improve stability, published builds may integrate Firebase Crashlytics (Google). Crashlytics collects crash reports and related technical data, such as:

  • stack traces and diagnostic signals;
  • basic device information and app version;
  • technical identifiers Google uses in its crash analytics infrastructure.

This data is intended to find and fix bugs; it is not meant to include your encrypted file contents or passwords. Information is transmitted according to Google/Firebase documentation and policies.

If a specific build does not include Crashlytics, this collection does not apply; you can review permissions and components of the installed build from the store listing or system app info.
